Day 3 | Tokyo

Tsukiji Fish Market

Take an early morning visit to Tsukiji market, the world’s biggest and busiest fish markets

TRAVEL TIP: If you’re an early bird and want to see something remarkable try to visit the tuna auction. The number of visitors to the tuna auction is limited to 120 per day, so you have to apply at the Osakana Fukyu Center (Fish Information Center) at the Kachidoki Gate, starting from 5:00am (or earlier on busy days) on a first-come, first-serve basis.

Otherwise just visit the outer market to find food related goods and fresh seafood in consumer quantities. I recommend grabbing a sushi breakfast/lunch nearby the market.

Ginza Area

Then if you are a shopaholic, head over to the Ginza area (one of the most luxurious shopping districts). I highly recommend checking out the flagship Uniqlo store (a gigantic 12 floor building). Also check out various shopping complexs such as Ginza Wako, Tokyu Plaza Ginza, Ginza Six. While in Ginza, you can also check out the Ginza Place (which has Nissan and Sony showrooms).

Dominique Ansel Bakery

In Ginza, check out Dominique Ansel Bakery for out of this world treats.

Optional

Yurakucho Gado-shita Dining

If you’re hungry, grab lunch at the Yurakucho Gado-shita Dining - an interesting dining district that is located beneath elevated train tracks.

Shinjuku district

Then head over to the Shinjuku district. Explore various department or electronics stores.

Robot Bar Restaurant

For a unique experience try the robot bar restaurant.
