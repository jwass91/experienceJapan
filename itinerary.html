Day 1: Arrive and start exploring Tokyo

Arrive in Tokyo and visit the Tokyo Skytree and Sensoji Temple. Then head over to Shibuya and explore Takeshita Street - enjoy the great shops and food.

View Itinerary for Day 1

Day 2: Traditional Tokyo

Discover the laid-back side of Tokyo in the Yanaka district. Then head over to Ueno to see some parks and museums. Then go and visit the Tokyo Imperial Palace.

View Full Itinerary for Day 2

Day 3: More fun in Tokyo

Take an early morning visit to the Tsukiji Fish Market (the world's biggest fish market). Then head over to the Ginza area for luxurious shopping. Take a pit-stop at Dominique Ansel Bakery for some amazing desserts. Grab lunch at Yurakucho Gado-shita - beneath elevated train tracks. Then head over to the Shinjuku district to shop and have a unique dinner at the Robot Bar resturant.

View Full Itinerary for Day 3
Optional

Day 4: Tech in Tokyo

If you are a techy or want to see some cool tech and have the extra day this is for you; if not skip to Day 5. This day is jam-packed with the coolest tech in Tokyo. You'll get to visit Akihabara Electric Town, the National Museum of Emerging Science and Innovation, the Toshiba Mueseum, the Sony Mueseum, and the Panasonic Showroom.

View Full Itinerary for Day 4

Day 5: Kyoto

Take the bullet train from Tokyo to Kyoto. Then head over to the Arashiyama Area to visit the Bamboo Groves, the Iwatayama Monkey Park, and the Adashino Temple. And if you have the time you can take a short trip north of Kyoto to experience hot springs.

View Full Itinerary for Day 5

Day 6: More Kyoto

Visit Nishiki Market (know as "Kyoto's Kitchen"), and Kiyomizudera Temple or Fushimi Inari Shrine.

View Full Itinerary for Day 6

Day 7: Nara

Take a day trip to Nara and visit Nara Koen Park - full of hundreds of roaming deer. Then head over to Todaiji Temple, the landmark of Nara.

View Full Itinerary for Day 7

Day 8: Osaka

If you want, you can go to Universal studios and/or Osaka Aquarium. You will also go to the Cup Noodles Museum Osaka, where you can make your own cup noodles. Then head over to the Minami (Namba) district for shopping and dinning. And visit Dotonbori street, a shopping and entertainment district that is also a food destination - at night it is lit with hundreds of neon lights and mechanized signs.

View Full Itinerary for Day 8
Option 1

Day 9-10: Niseko

This is one of your options for Days 9 and 10 (only recommended if you are going between December and February). Take a flight up north to Hokkaido and head over to Niseko to hit the slopes. You'll get two days to ski and can experience an Igloo if you want.

View Full Itinerary for Niseko
Option 2

Day 9-10: Hiroshima

This is your other option for Days 9 and 10. Arrive in Hiroshima (via bullet train) and visit Hiroshima's Peace Memorial Park. Here you'll visit the Peace Memorial Museum, which surveys the history of Hiroshima and the advent of the nuclear bomb. You will also see the A-Bomb Dome, also known as the Hiroshima Peace Memorial, which is what remains of the former Prefectural Industrial Promotion Hall. You can also visit the Mazda Museum and/or Hiroshima Castle. Then you will head to Downtown Hiroshima and snack on some Hiroshima Style Okonomiyaki (a fried snack). For the Day 10, you will take a day trip to Miyajima, a small island near Hiroshima. Here you will see the torii gate of the Itsukushima Shrine.
