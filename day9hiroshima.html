Day 9 | Hiroshima

Sakura Shinkansen

Using your JR Pass take the Sakura shinkansen (bullet train) from Osaka station to Hiroshima station. This ride is about 1 and a half hours.

Hiroshima Peace Park

After in Hiroshima, head over to Hiroshima Peace Park - one of the most prominent features of the city. Here, you should visit the Peace Memorial Museum, which surveys the history of Hiroshima and the advent of the nuclear bomb. You will also see the A-Bomb Dome, also known as the Hiroshima Peace Memorial, which is what remains of the former Prefectural Industrial Promotion Hall.

Optional

Mazda Museum

You can take a local train to the Mazda Museum. The Mazda motor company was founded in Hiroshima and its headquarters are still in Hiroshima. The museum is a portion of Mazda's headquarters that is open to the public

Hiroshima Castle

Hiroshima Castle is a 15 minute walk from Peace Park. The castle is 5 stories tall and surrounded by a moat.

Downtown Hiroshima - try some Okonomiyaki

Spend your night in downtown Hiroshima. Make sure you to visit Hondori Street and also try some Okonomiyaki (a delicious fried food), which Hiroshima is famous for.
