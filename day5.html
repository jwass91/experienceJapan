Day 5 | Kyoto

Hikari Shinkansen

Using your JR Pass take Hikari Shinkansen (bullet train) on the Tokaido Shinkansen line from Tokyo Station to Kyoto Station. With your JR Pass you can reserve a seat and ride for free (rather than spending 13,500 yen). The Hikari line has very few stops so it will take around an hour and forty minutes to get from Tokyo to Kyoto.

Arashiyama Area

Take the JR San-in line from Kyoto Station to Saga-Arashiyama Station (about 40 mins).

Bamboo Groves

In the Arashiyama Area, vist the Bamboo Grooves. The groves are particularly pretty when there is a light wind, causing the tall bamboo stalks to gently sway back and forth.

Monkey Park Iwatayama

The Monkey Park is located in the Arashiyama mountains (the entrance can be found just south of the Togetsukyo Bridge). After a short hike uphill, you will find an open area with over a hundred monkeys roaming freely.

Adashino Temple

While in Arashiyama, you should also check out the Adashino Temple (located at the end of the Saga-Toriimoto Preserved Street). The temple is covered hundreds in stone statues for the souls of the dead here.

Optional

Kurama Onsen

If your up for it, after getting back to central Kyoto, head 30 minutes north of Kyoto to experience Onsen (hot springs). You can relax while sitting in the outdoor tub surrounded by forest and mountains.
