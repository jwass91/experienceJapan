Day 1 | Tokyo

Arrive at Narita Airport

Fly into Tokyo's international Narita Airport (NRT). Using your JR Pass take the Narita Express to Tokyo station in the center of Tokyo.

JR Pass?

The Japan Rail Pass provides unlimited travel on the entire national JR network, including the high speed bullet train lines. I highly recommend getting the JR Pass - you will be taking a lot of trains and the JR Pass will save you a ton.

Tokyo Skytree

From Tokyo Station take a bus to Tokyo Skytree (the tallest structure in Japan) for an amazing view of Tokyo.

TRAVEL TIP: I recommend buying the Skytree Fast Ticket Combo Ticket for access to the first and second observatories decks. The Skytree Fast Ticket Combo costs 4000 yen (approx. $37). Skytree Fast Tickets are a special type of ticket available only to foreign tourists that allow holders to skip the lines. (*PASSPORT REQUIRED*).

Sensoji Temple

From Tokyo Skytree Station take the Tobu Skytree Line to Asakusa Station and then walk to Sensoji (Tokyo's oldest temple). In between gates of the temple is a shopping street called Nakamise so pick up some souvenirs and snacks.

Shibuya

From the Asakusa Station take the Ginza line to Shibuya Station to end your day. Shibuya has great shops and nightlife so I recommend grabbing dinner here.

Takeshita Street

In Shibuya head over to Takeshita Street - lined with trendy shops fashion boutiques and trendy fast food outlets.

Totti Candy Factory

On Takeshtia street I recommend going to Totti Candy Factory for a gigantic cotton candy.

Optional

Harry Hedgehodge Cafe

Nearby check out Harry Hedgehodge Cafe to see some adorable hedgehodges.
